What is Savings Planner?

Savings Planner is a web application that helps you plan your savings by providing you with investment options and suggestions. Here are some investment options

Bonds


A government bond or sovereign bond is a form of bond issued by a government to support public spending. It generally includes a commitment to pay periodic interest, called coupon payments, and to repay the face value on the maturity date.

Treasury Bills


A Treasury bill (T-Bill) is a short-term government debt obligation backed by the Bank of Canada with a maturity of one year or less. Treasury bills are usually sold in denominations of $1,000. However, some can reach a maximum denomination of $5 million in non-competitive bids. These securities are widely regarded as low-risk and secure investments.

Bitcoin (BTC)


Bitcoin is a decentralized digital currency and is the most well-known cryptocurrency in the world. It is recorded in a public distributed ledger called a blockchain. While it is popular, it is highly volatile. Bitcoin can double its value in a matter of weeks and can also drop in more than 50% of its value in a matter of days.

SPDR S&P 500 ETF


The SPDR S&P 500 ETF trust is an exchange-traded fund that trades on the NYSE Arca under the symbol SPY. It is designed to track the S&P 500 stock market index. This fund is the largest and oldest ETF in the world.

Real Estate


Real Estate refers to residential real estate within the GTA. These are medium-risk investments that are highly dependent on the current housing market.
